## Definition

**Beta** ($\beta$) measures the sensitivity of an asset's returns to a benchmark (usually the market):

$$
\beta_i = \frac{\text{Cov}(r_i, r_m)}{\text{Var}(r_m)} = \rho_{i,m} \cdot \frac{\sigma_i}{\sigma_m}
$$

## Interpretation

| Beta | Interpretation |
|------|---------------|
| $\beta = 0$ | No market exposure (cash-like) |
| $\beta = 1$ | Moves exactly with the market |
| $\beta > 1$ | Amplifies market moves (e.g., growth stocks) |
| $\beta < 0$ | Inverse of market (e.g., VIX, gold in some periods) |

## Estimating Beta

Simple OLS regression of asset returns on market returns:

$$
r_i = \alpha + \beta r_m + \epsilon
$$

In Python:

```python
import numpy as np

def estimate_beta(asset_returns, market_returns):
    cov = np.cov(asset_returns, market_returns)
    return cov[0, 1] / cov[1, 1]
```

## Limitations

- **Non-stationary**: Beta changes over time, especially during crises
- **Depends on estimation window**: 1-year vs 5-year betas can differ dramatically  
- **Single-factor assumption**: True exposure involves many factors, not just the market
