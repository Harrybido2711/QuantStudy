## The Order Book

An **order book** is a real-time list of buy (bid) and sell (ask) orders at different price levels.

```
Ask side (sellers):
  $100.05 × 500
  $100.02 × 200  ← best ask
Spread: $0.02
  $100.00 × 300  ← best bid
  $99.98  × 1000
Bid side (buyers)
```

The **bid-ask spread** is the difference between best ask and best bid. It represents the cost of immediacy.

## Order Types

| Order | Execution | Risk |
|-------|-----------|------|
| **Market Order** | Immediate, best available price | Market impact, slippage |
| **Limit Order** | Only at specified price or better | May not fill (non-execution risk) |
| **Stop Order** | Triggers market order at stop price | Slippage past stop level |
| **Iceberg Order** | Shows only partial quantity | Reduced market impact |

## Bid-Ask Spread Decomposition

The spread compensates market makers for:

$$
\text{Spread} = \text{Adverse Selection Cost} + \text{Inventory Cost} + \text{Operating Cost}
$$

**Adverse selection**: Trading against informed traders who know something you don't.

## Market Impact

When you execute a large order, you move the price against yourself. The **price impact** model:

$$
\Delta P = \sigma \cdot \sqrt{\frac{Q}{ADV}} \cdot \eta
$$

Where:
- $Q$ = order quantity
- $ADV$ = average daily volume
- $\sigma$ = daily volatility
- $\eta$ = market impact coefficient (~0.1–0.5)

## Implementation Shortfall

The total cost of trading vs. the arrival price:

$$
IS = \underbrace{(\bar{P}_\text{exec} - P_\text{arrival}) \cdot Q}_\text{market impact} + \underbrace{(\text{signed timing moves})}_\text{timing risk}
$$

Optimal execution (TWAP vs VWAP vs Almgren-Chriss) trades off market impact against timing risk.
