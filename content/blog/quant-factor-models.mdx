## What Is a Factor Model?

A factor model decomposes asset returns into **systematic** (factor-driven) and **idiosyncratic** components:

$$
r_i = \alpha_i + \sum_{k=1}^{K} \beta_{ik} f_k + \epsilon_i
$$

Where:
- $r_i$ = return of asset $i$
- $\alpha_i$ = alpha (abnormal return)
- $\beta_{ik}$ = factor loading (exposure)
- $f_k$ = factor return
- $\epsilon_i$ = idiosyncratic residual, $\mathbb{E}[\epsilon_i] = 0$

The key assumption: $\text{Cov}(\epsilon_i, \epsilon_j) = 0$ for $i \neq j$.

---

## CAPM: The Single-Factor Model

The Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM) is the simplest factor model with one factor — the market:

$$
\mathbb{E}[r_i] - r_f = \beta_i (\mathbb{E}[r_m] - r_f)
$$

Where $\beta_i = \frac{\text{Cov}(r_i, r_m)}{\text{Var}(r_m)}$.

**Empirical failures of CAPM:**
- Small-cap premium (Banz, 1981)
- Value premium (Fama-French, 1992)
- Momentum (Jegadeesh-Titman, 1993)
- Low volatility anomaly

---

## Fama-French Three-Factor Model

Fama and French added two factors to explain the small-cap and value premia:

$$
r_i - r_f = \alpha + \beta_\text{mkt} (r_m - r_f) + \beta_\text{SMB} \cdot SMB + \beta_\text{HML} \cdot HML + \epsilon
$$

| Factor | Name | Construction |
|--------|------|-------------|
| MKT    | Market | $r_m - r_f$ |
| SMB    | Small Minus Big | Small-cap returns − Large-cap returns |
| HML    | High Minus Low | High B/M − Low B/M returns |

The **five-factor model** (2015) adds **RMW** (Robust Minus Weak profitability) and **CMA** (Conservative Minus Aggressive investment).

## Barra-Style Risk Models

In practice, risk models like MSCI Barra decompose portfolio variance:

$$
\text{Var}(r_p) = \mathbf{x}^T (BFB^T + \Delta) \mathbf{x}
$$

Where:
- $\mathbf{x}$ = portfolio weights
- $B$ = factor exposure matrix ($N \times K$)
- $F$ = factor covariance matrix ($K \times K$)
- $\Delta$ = diagonal idiosyncratic variance matrix

This allows portfolio managers to **attribute risk** to specific factors.

```python
import numpy as np

def portfolio_risk(weights, B, F, delta):
    """Compute portfolio variance via factor model."""
    factor_var = weights @ B @ F @ B.T @ weights
    idio_var = weights @ np.diag(delta) @ weights
    return factor_var + idio_var
```

## Alpha vs Risk Factors

The crucial distinction in quant finance:

**Risk factors** explain cross-sectional return differences as compensation for bearing systematic risk. **Alpha** is return above and beyond risk factor exposure — the holy grail.

$$
\alpha_i = r_i - \sum_k \hat{\beta}_{ik} \hat{f}_k
$$

A common mistake is confusing **factor exposure** (beta) with **alpha**. Value investing isn't alpha if you're just loading on HML.

---

## Information Ratio

The quality of an alpha signal is measured by the **Information Ratio**:

$$
IR = \frac{\mathbb{E}[\alpha]}{\sigma(\alpha)} = IC \cdot \sqrt{N}
$$

Where $IC$ is the Information Coefficient (correlation between predicted and realized returns) and $N$ is the number of independent bets. This is the **Fundamental Law of Active Management** (Grinold, 1989).
